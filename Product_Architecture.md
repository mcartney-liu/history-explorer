# History Explorer - Product Architecture

Version: 1.0

Status: Active


# 1. Overview

History Explorer is an AI-powered historical exploration platform.

The product architecture is designed around:

Explore → Connect → Understand → Discover


The system enables users to explore historical knowledge through time, space, entities, and AI interpretation.


# 2. Product Architecture Layers


## Layer 1: User Exploration Layer

Purpose:

Provide the main user experience.


Capabilities:

- Search and discovery entry points.
- Timeline exploration.
- Geographic exploration.
- Entity exploration.
- Exploration path navigation.


Users interact with history through:

- Events.
- People.
- Civilizations.
- Locations.
- Time periods.


---

## Layer 2: Historical Knowledge Layer

Purpose:

Organize historical knowledge into connected structures.


Core entities:

### Event

Historical occurrences.

Examples:

- Wars.
- Political changes.
- Scientific discoveries.
- Cultural movements.


### Person

Historical individuals.


### Civilization

Human societies and cultural systems.


### Location

Geographic context.


### Time Period

Historical timeline structure.


Relationships:

Entities should be connected through meaningful historical relationships.


---

## Layer 3: AI Exploration Layer

Purpose:

Provide intelligent historical guidance.


AI capabilities:

- Historical explanation.
- Relationship discovery.
- Context generation.
- Exploration suggestions.
- Question answering.


AI role:

Guide users through exploration.

AI is not the final authority of historical truth.


---

## Layer 4: Exploration Visualization Layer

Purpose:

Make complex historical relationships understandable.


Visualization methods:

- Timeline.
- Map.
- Relationship graph.
- Interactive knowledge network.


---

## Layer 5: Knowledge Expansion Layer

Purpose:

Support continuous growth.


Capabilities:

- New historical data integration.
- Knowledge updates.
- Community and expert contributions.
- Future AI-assisted expansion.


# 3. Core Product Flow


User starts:

Historical topic


↓

Explore entities


↓

Discover relationships


↓

Understand context


↓

Continue exploration


# 4. Core Product Model


History Explorer is built around four dimensions:


## Time

When did it happen?


## Space

Where did it happen?


## Entity

Who or what participated?


## Relationship

How are things connected?


# 5. Product Architecture Principles


1. Exploration first.
2. Connections over isolated information.
3. AI as interpretation layer.
4. Human understanding over information quantity.
5. Scalable knowledge structure.
