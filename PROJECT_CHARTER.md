# History Explorer - Project Charter

Version: 1.0

Status: Active


# 1. Project Mission

History Explorer aims to build an AI-powered global history exploration platform.

The project mission is to transform history learning from passive information consumption into active exploration and discovery.

The product helps users understand historical events, relationships, causes, and impacts through AI-powered exploration.


# 2. Project Vision

Build a new generation of historical exploration experience.

Users should not only find historical information, but also discover connections between:

- Events
- People
- Civilizations
- Locations
- Time periods


# 3. Problem Statement

Traditional history products have several limitations:

- Information is fragmented.
- Historical relationships are difficult to discover.
- Users often know what happened but struggle to understand why it happened.
- Time and geographic connections are difficult to explore.


History Explorer addresses these problems through connected historical knowledge exploration.


# 4. Product Scope

The project focuses on:

- Historical knowledge exploration.
- AI-assisted historical interpretation.
- Timeline exploration.
- Geographic exploration.
- Relationship discovery between historical entities.


The project does NOT aim to become:

- A simple encyclopedia.
- A digital textbook.
- A general-purpose chatbot.
- A social media platform.
- A news platform.


# 5. Success Criteria

Project success is measured by:

- Users can easily begin historical exploration.
- Users can discover meaningful historical connections.
- Users can understand historical causes and consequences.
- The platform can continuously expand historical knowledge.


# 6. Project Principles

The project follows these principles:

1. Exploration over searching.
2. Connections over isolated information.
3. Understanding over memorization.
4. Long-term scalability over short-term implementation speed.
5. Documentation before implementation.


# 7. Governance

## Product Owner

Responsible for:

- Product decisions.
- Priority decisions.
- Final approval.


## Product Architect

Responsible for:

- Product architecture.
- Documentation system.
- Design review.
- AI collaboration standards.


## AI Agent

Responsible for:

- Implementation.
- Testing.
- Repository operations.

AI Agents must not change product direction without approval.


# 8. Decision Authority

Final product decisions belong to the Product Owner.

Product Architect provides recommendations and design guidance.

AI Agents execute approved tasks.
